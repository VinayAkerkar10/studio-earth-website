Coastal Retreat

Weekend home, Alibaug, Maharashtra

Client: Naina Kapoor

A beachside home designed for breeze, shade, and an easy dialogue between inside and out.

Project Overview

A Relaxed Coastal Home Framed by Wind and Light

Coastal Retreat was imagined as a weekend home that could feel open to the landscape without becoming exposed to heat, glare, or salt-laden winds. The architecture uses deep overhangs, shaded verandas, and carefully aligned openings to encourage natural airflow throughout the day.

Instead of relying on decorative gestures, the house is defined by proportion, texture, and a calm relationship between interior rooms and outdoor terraces. Shared spaces open easily to the sea breeze, while private bedrooms remain more sheltered and intimate.

The interiors are pared back and tactile, with finishes chosen for comfort and durability near the coast. Every decision, from window placement to material tone, supports a slower rhythm of living that feels informal, grounded, and quietly luxurious.

Project Details

  • Location: Weekend home, Alibaug, Maharashtra
  • Client: Naina Kapoor
  • Focus: Coastal comfort, shade, and indoor-outdoor living
  • Language: Breezy planning, warm finishes, and low-maintenance materials
Key Highlights

Design Intent

  • Deep shaded edges soften the transition from landscape to interior.
  • Cross ventilation reduces reliance on mechanical cooling through most of the year.
  • Open-plan common spaces support casual gathering and seasonal living.
  • A robust material palette is chosen to age gracefully in the coastal climate.
