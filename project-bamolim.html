House In Bamolim

A home shaped by sea views, sloping roofs, and a quiet material palette rooted in place.

Project Overview

A Sea-Facing Home Defined by Simplicity and Craft

House In Bamolim is a holiday home designed around the quiet beauty of the Arabian Sea and the changing light of Goa. Set on a slope, the architecture uses careful placement, large windows, and sliding doors to draw the view deep into everyday life, ensuring every room remains connected to the landscape.

The design takes cues from Goan architecture through sloping roofs, eaves boards, and a grounded sense of shelter, while subtle Bengali influences appear in Alpana artwork and traditional wooden louvered windows. The result is a home that feels regionally rooted yet minimal in expression.

Interiors remain restrained and elegant, allowing proportion, texture, and craftsmanship to carry the experience. Materials such as Accoya wood and Burma teak were selected for durability and sustainability, while antique lighting sourced from Mumbai introduces warmth and an old-world depth to the spaces.

Project Details

  • Location: Holiday home, Bambolim, Goa
  • Client: Mithu Alur & Sathi Alur
  • Focus: Simplicity, sustainability, and sea views
  • Materials: Accoya wood, Burma teak, handcrafted finishes
Key Highlights

What Shapes the Project

  • Sea views are framed from every room through carefully placed openings.
  • Sloping roofs and eaves respond to the climate while echoing Goan domestic architecture.
  • Bengali cultural references appear through artwork and traditional window details.
  • Natural materials and antique lighting create warmth, longevity, and emotional depth.
