Urban Villa

Private residence, Bengaluru, Karnataka

Client: Ananya & Rohan Mehta

A contemporary family home shaped by privacy, filtered light, and quiet spatial continuity.

Project Overview

A Minimal Home for City Life and Everyday Calm

Urban Villa was designed for a compact city neighborhood where privacy, daylight, and ventilation needed to work together rather than compete. The architecture uses a clear massing strategy, recessed openings, and layered thresholds to create a sense of calm within a dense urban setting.

The planning prioritizes fluid family living, with shared spaces arranged around light-filled voids and quieter private zones set deeper into the plan. Materials are restrained and warm, allowing the architecture to feel modern without becoming severe or overly polished.

Throughout the house, built-in storage, carefully framed views, and natural finishes support a lifestyle that values simplicity and function. The result is a home that feels composed, intimate, and deeply supportive of daily routines.

Project Details

  • Location: Private residence, Bengaluru, Karnataka
  • Client: Ananya & Rohan Mehta
  • Focus: Privacy, daylight, and minimal family living
  • Language: Clean lines, warm textures, and efficient planning
Key Highlights

Design Intent

  • Layered facades balance openness with privacy on a busy urban edge.
  • A clear circulation spine connects common and private spaces with ease.
  • Natural light is introduced through courtyards, skylights, and deep openings.
  • A restrained material palette gives the house warmth without visual clutter.
