Compact City Home

Townhouse, Pune, Maharashtra

Client: Samar & Leena Shah

A small-plot residence where light, storage, and spatial efficiency define the experience of home.

Project Overview

A Small Home That Feels Open, Ordered, and Adaptable

Compact City Home responds to a tight urban plot by treating space as something to be layered rather than simply divided. The planning focuses on visual openness, natural light, and carefully integrated storage so the house feels generous despite its modest footprint.

Instead of relying on large rooms, the design creates flexibility through multipurpose areas, connected sightlines, and furniture that supports different routines through the day. Openings are placed to capture light and ventilation while maintaining comfort and privacy from neighboring buildings.

The interior language remains simple and durable, with warm finishes softening the compact plan. The result is a house that works hard in practical terms while still feeling calm, uncluttered, and deeply personal for a growing family.

Project Details

  • Location: Townhouse, Pune, Maharashtra
  • Client: Samar & Leena Shah
  • Focus: Efficient planning, storage, and natural light
  • Language: Compact living with warm minimal interiors
Key Highlights

Design Intent

  • Storage is integrated into walls, seating, and circulation zones to reduce clutter.
  • Split-level relationships help define functions without heavy visual separation.
  • A carefully lit core keeps the house bright even on a narrow site.
  • Simple detailing and durable materials make the home easy to maintain over time.
